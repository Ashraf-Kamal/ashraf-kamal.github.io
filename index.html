Bibliography

I am currently a Professor in the Department of Computer Science at Stanford University. My research journey spans over 15 years, during which I have made significant contributions to the fields of Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning, and Natural Language Processing.

Prior to joining Stanford, I served as an Associate Professor at MIT (2015-2020) and completed my postdoctoral research at Oxford University. I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from Carnegie Mellon University in 2012, where I worked on developing novel deep learning architectures for complex pattern recognition tasks.

I have authored over 150 peer-reviewed publications in top-tier conferences and journals, including NeurIPS, ICML, ACL, and Nature Machine Intelligence. My work has been recognized with several prestigious awards, and I have been invited to deliver keynote speeches at major international conferences. I am passionate about advancing AI research while ensuring its ethical and responsible development for societal benefit.
